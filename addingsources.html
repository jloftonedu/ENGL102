What are Secondary Sources?

Secondary sources analyze, interpret, or comment on primary sources. They provide scholarly perspective and context to support your arguments.

Good Secondary Sources:

  • • Scholarly books
  • • Peer-reviewed research
  • • Expert analysis

Avoid These:

  • • Wikipedia as a final source
  • • Personal blogs
  • • Non-academic websites
  • • Outdated sources (unless historical)

1. Direct Quote
